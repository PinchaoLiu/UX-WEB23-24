Hello human, nice to meet you. This is Mr. Pomelo's profile.


Name: Mr. Pomelo
Family name: Rutaceae
Genus: Citrus
Born in: 800 BC
Race: Asian plant
Height: 14m
Skills: Make cuisine(Pomelo Vinegar, Honey Pomelo Tea), perfume and medicine(Treatment of senile cough and asthma, cough, frostbite, headache, arthralgia, acute mastitis.)
Family members (children): Sweet Orange, Bitter Orange, Tangelo, Grapefruit


Growth characteristics and life cycle:
Tree. Young branches, leaf undersides, flower stalks, sepals, and ovaries are all covered with soft hair. Young leaves are usually dark purplish-red, and young branches are flat with edges. The leaves are thick, dark green, broadly ovate or elliptic, including petioles, 9-16 centimeters long, 4-8 centimeters wide, or larger, with blunt or round tips, sometimes slightly pointed, and rounded bases. Wing leaves are 2-4 centimeters long, 0.5-3 centimeters wide, with some varieties having very narrow wing leaves.
Inflorescences are terminal, occasionally with axillary solitary flowers; flower buds are pale purplish-red, rarely milky white; sepals irregularly 5-3 shallowly lobed; petals 1.5-2 centimeters long; stamens 25-35, with some stamens occasionally sterile; pistils stout, with stigmas slightly larger than the ovaries.
Fruits are spherical, flattened, pear-shaped, or broadly conical, usually with a horizontal diameter of over 10 centimeters, pale yellow or yellow-green, with hybrid varieties having crimson hues. Fruit skins are quite thick or thin, spongy, with large oil cells, protruding, with a solid yet soft fruit core. Rinds consist of 10-15 or even up to 19 segments, pulp cells are white, pink, or bright red, occasionally with a milky yellow hue. Seeds can number up to 200, irregularly shaped, usually resembling rectangles, thin and often truncate at the upper part, plump at the lower part, sometimes incomplete development, evident longitudinal ribs, cotyledons are milky white, single embryo.
Flowering occurs from April to May, and fruiting from September to December.
